AI: The **Paderborn University (PU) Bearing Fault Diagnosis Dataset** is a widely used benchmark dataset in the field of rotating machinery fault diagnosis, condition monitoring, and predictive maintenance. Developed by the Paderborn University in Germany, it provides comprehensive vibration and current signal data from bearings under various fault conditions and operating scenarios.

### Key Features of the PU Dataset:

- **Fault Types**: The dataset includes multiple types of bearing faults, such as:
  - Inner race faults (e.g., KI04, KI14, KI16, etc.)
  - Outer race faults (e.g., KA04, KA15, KA22, etc.)
  - Rolling element faults
  - Multiple faults (e.g., combined inner and outer race damage)

- **Bearing Conditions**: The dataset comprises **14 distinct bearing classes**, categorized into:
  - **6 healthy (normal) bearings**
  - **12 artificially induced faults** (e.g., inner or outer race damage)
  - **14 real-world damaged bearings** from accelerated life testing (including outer race, inner race, and multiple faults)

- **Operating Conditions (Domains)**: Four different operating conditions are defined, varying:
  - Rotational speed: 900 rpm (N09) or 1500 rpm (N15)
  - Load torque: 0.1 Nm (M01) or 0.7 Nm (M07)
  - Radial load: 400 N (F04) or 1000 N (F10)

  These conditions are labeled as:
  - N09_M07_F10
  - N15_M01_F10
  - N15_M07_F04
  - N15_M07_F10

  Each condition is repeated **20 times**, resulting in 20 samples per fault type and condition.

- **Data Collection**:
  - **Sampling frequency**: 64 kHz
  - **Duration per sample**: 4 seconds
  - **Signal types**: Vibration signals (measured via high-precision accelerometers) and current signals (from motor current sensors)
  - **Data format**: Stored in `.mat` files, with time-series data for one or more channels

- **Data Structure**:
  - Each bearing has **20 segments** of data (4 seconds each), leading to **400 data samples per bearing** when sliced into 2048-point segments.
  - Total dataset size: Up to **7,600 Ã— 2048** data points (used in some studies for training and testing).

- **Experimental Setup**:
  - The test bench includes five main components: torque shaft, bearing test module, flywheel, motor, and load motor.
  - Faults are induced through both **artificial damage** and **accelerated life testing**, ensuring real-world relevance.

- **Applications**:
  - Used for developing and evaluating **machine learning** and **deep learning** models for fault diagnosis.
  - Widely adopted in **domain generalization**, **transfer learning**, and **semi-supervised learning** tasks due to its multi-condition structure.
  - Applied in research on **anomaly detection**, **feature extraction**, and **robust fault classification**.

### Usage in Research:
- The dataset is commonly used in studies involving **deep learning**, **convolutional neural networks (CNNs)**, **quadratic neural networks (QCNNs)**, and **positive-unlabeled (PU) learning**.
- It has been employed in benchmarking **transfer learning** and **domain generalization** methods, particularly in scenarios where models must generalize across different operating conditions.

### Summary:

It serves as a critical resource for advancing intelligent fault detection systems in industrial applications.
